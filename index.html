Delivery service accessibility in USA

GIS for Design Practices Kitae & Julia

Package Delivery

There are numerous package delivery service providers across the United States, including UPS, USPS, FedEx, and Amazon, all with widespread distribution. While companies like UPS operate in most regions, Amazon's delivery services are primarily concentrated in large cities and along major highways.

Grocery Delivery

Across the United States, numerous grocery brands offer delivery services within a radius of up to 15 miles. Walmart leads with 5,234 locations and the broadest coverage, followed by Target with 1,780 locations, Trader Joe's with 521, and Whole Foods with 517.

Meal Delivery

Meal delivery services are experiencing continuous growth, with most deliveries made by bicycle or car. These services primarily operate in urban areas, depending on the proximity of delivery personnel and restaurants. DoorDash covers smaller service areas, while UberEats operates across the largest regions.

Delivery Service Gaps and Population Density

Most regions in the U.S. that lack delivery services are located in geographically challenging areas, such as deserts or mountains. However, even within certain distances from city centers, there are areas that can be defined as "delivery deserts," where no delivery services are available.

Delivery Desert

Approximately 4 million households in the U.S. live in food deserts, where access to grocery stores is limited. In these areas, residents may rely on delivery services as an alternative to obtain food. However, many regions in the U.S. still lack both grocery and meal delivery options, eaving some food deserts without adequate access to fresh food.
